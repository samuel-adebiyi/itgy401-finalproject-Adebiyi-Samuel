## Audience and Job

The primary audience is small businesses, founders, and student-led organisations that need understandable, affordable technology support. The website's main job is to explain services and convert a visitor into a well-formed project enquiry.

## Required Pages

1. `index.html`: value proposition, services overview, process, and call to action.
2. `about.html`: founder profile, principles, skills, and working approach.
3. `services.html`: service catalogue, comparison table, and pricing guidance.
4. `contact.html`: enquiry form, contact details, and frequently asked questions.

## Services

- Website design and development
- Business technology support
- Data and reporting setup
- Digital consultation and planning

Any displayed prices are starting estimates for demonstration and must not imply a binding quotation.

## Interaction Contracts

- Mobile navigation opens and closes accessibly.
- Service selection can carry into the contact form.
- The enquiry form validates required fields and displays an in-page confirmation; it must state that this student demonstration does not transmit data to a server.
- No control may be a no-op.

## Visual and Content Standard

The design should feel credible, modern, calm, and distinctly technical without looking like a generic neon startup template. Copy must be concise and specific. The site must work from 320px mobile width through large desktop screens.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Four linked semantic HTML pages.
- Consistent navigation, design system, and footer.
- External stylesheet and external JavaScript.
- Responsive layouts across mobile, tablet, and desktop.
- Accessible form with appropriate input types.
- Accessible table containing structured service information.
- At least one purposeful JavaScript feature.
- Clean project structure and GitHub Pages compatibility.
